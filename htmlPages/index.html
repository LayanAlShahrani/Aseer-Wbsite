Al-Qatt Al-Asiri

is a traditional decorative art that originated in the Asir region in southern Saudi Arabia and is considered one of the most prominent traditional women’s arts. This art is characterized by the use of vibrant colors such as red, blue, green, and yellow, and relies on geometric patterns including triangles, squares, and straight lines. Women in the Asir region were responsible for decorating the walls of homes, especially in the “majlis” (guest rooms), reflecting their role in preserving the local heritage and culture. Al-Qatt Al-Asiri is not only an aesthetic art but also carries cultural dimensions that express the Asir identity and community values. In 2017, this art was inscribed on UNESCO’s Intangible Cultural Heritage List, highlighting its historical importance and the need to preserve it. In modern times, Al-Qatt Al-Asiri has gained increased attention and has been integrated into contemporary designs, from art pieces to furniture, alongside efforts to pass it on to future generations through art workshops and cultural events

Attire in Aseer

Women’s Attire in Aseer

  • Masraf or Daraa: This is a long, loose garment worn by women, often adorned with colorful embroidery featuring geometric patterns that reflect local heritage
  • Masraf or Daraa: Women’s clothing in Asir is distinguished by bright colors such as red, green, and yellow. Hand embroidery is frequently used to embellish sleeves and collars, adding an artistic touch to the attire
  • Masraf or Daraa: Women complement their outfits with traditional jewelry, including necklaces, bracelets, and earrings, often made from silver or gold, highlighting the beauty and elegance of the attire

Men’s Attire in Aseer

  • Asiri Thobe: The thobe is characterized by its length and loose fit, typically made from lightweight fabrics suitable for the region’s climate. It may feature simple embroidery on the sleeves or edges
  • M’awz: This is a wrap worn around the waist, and it is a part of the traditional attire. It is distinguished by its colorful designs and is often decorated
  • anbiah: The janbiah, or traditional dagger, is an important part of men’s dress, worn around the waist, symbolizing masculinity and bravery
